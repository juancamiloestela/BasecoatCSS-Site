Forms| Let your users push your buttons

  • Buttons

Forms are the way users interact with your site, therefore they should be as readable and efficient as possible. Basecoat offer a set of predefined form layouts along with typographically coherent elements to allow you to build forms that will engage your audience.

Buttons

Buttons can be traditional <button> tags or links with the class "button". Buttons are also styled according to the typography and will be sized and vertically aligned according to their container and surrounding text.

Some form elements vary their style and rendering between operating systems and browsers. This is why Basecoat provides an ultra lightweight jQuery plugin to skin checkboxes, radio buttons and selects with a normalized skin which you can extend, while retaining accessibility via keyboard, mouse or touch events. To see more information about these plugins check out the sections for Checkboxjs and Selectjs in the Components page. All selects are skinned by default, no class is needed.

Forms

Forms will allow you to layout your forms in several ways according to your needs. The provided styles should get you up and running in no time and offer time tested layouts that ensure your users will follow the form requirements without scaring them away. Because you know, we love our users.

Form inputs are wrapped in a contianer with class="form-field" and optional stacked, inline or mirrored to control their presentation. These optional clases can also go in the parent form and all fields in it will conform to that style.

Form Feedback

Form feedback are the messages or states a form can be in to tell the user something, amongst these are alert messages, error messages and error states. Keep in mind that error, success and caution messages inside each form-field are by default hidden so no need to ad and remove stuff via js. Only set the desired class name and the correct message and color decorations will appear.
