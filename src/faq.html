Faq intro

Why oh why another CSS framework?!
lol
I know...
The thing is that after years of developing, client's needs were often so custom that using Bootstrap or Foundation eventually required tons of styles to override the ones provided by the framework, sometimes even having to fight against predefined !importants (headbang)...
So I went over all my work over the previous years in front end and recompiled and updated a set of styles which became Basecoat, a framework that aims to be as simple, yet as beautiful as possible, and that empowers you to build on top of it :)
Are you saying that I shouldn't use Bootstrap or Foundation?
Not at all!
those are amazing projects made by awesome developers which served as an inspiration for Basecoat. They are great for prototypes or projects where visual design is not so custom, but they are not necessarily the best choice for every single project.

Why is it called "Basecoat"?
When artists paint on a canvas, they first apply a basecoat of primer to it. This basecoat serves as a neutral color and as a structure for further paint coats to glide over the canvas and provide support that allows the painting to stand the test of time. A paint coat can be the background colors, the highlights or a clear protective coating in the end.
This is exactly the purpose of this framework, to provide the least amount of style possible that offers an aesthetically pleasing result that can be easily enhanced with few additional styles.
What are coats?
Short answer... coats are themes, but a special kind of theme.
They can be mixed and stacked on top of each other to match your needs.
As with art, in css you can apply coats to basecoat. These coats can make your elements rounded, change the color palette, change the typography or even add a functionality like a date picker.
Won't having many coats make my site slow?
Yes, any site with too much css and/or js will eventually get slow. That is why Basecoat aims to be as simple an minimalistic as possible so that you can add a couple of coats on top of it.
This approach ensures that the styles you add via coats are styles you need. Instead of a huge framework with tons of styles you will not use or will have a hard time overwriting. In every case, concatenating Basecoat and its coats is highly recommended.
But I need "____" feature, will you make it?
Additional features in Basecoat will come by demand and by evolution of technology. For example...
grids will eventually be replaced by flexbox layouts when they are widely supported.
But an image slider despite being a common feature, is highly improbable to match everyone's specific needs so it is better that another plugin or coat be added on a per project basis.
